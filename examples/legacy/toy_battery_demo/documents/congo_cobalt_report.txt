USGS Mineral Commodity Summary — Cobalt (2025)

World mine production of cobalt in 2024 was approximately 230,000 metric tons.
The Democratic Republic of the Congo (DRC) continued to dominate global cobalt
mine production, accounting for approximately 73% of world output. Australia
was the second-largest producer at approximately 3%, followed by the Philippines
at approximately 3%. Indonesia has been rapidly increasing production, reaching
approximately 3% of global output.

China controlled approximately 75% of global cobalt refining capacity, processing
ore imported primarily from the DRC. Finland and Belgium each held about 5% of
refining capacity.
