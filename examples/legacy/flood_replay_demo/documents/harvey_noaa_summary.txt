EXCERPT:

Hurricane Harvey is the first Category 4 (and first major) hurricane to make landfall along the Middle Texas Coast since Celia in 1970. Harvey exploded rapidly from a tropical depression to a major hurricane in around 40 hours. After impacting the Yucatan Peninsula earlier in the month as a tropical storm, Harvey moved into the warm waters of the Gulf of America late on Tuesday August 22nd.

On Thursday August 24th, Harvey's impact on the Middle and Upper Texas Coast seemed almost certain and potentially devastating. Not only was Harvey forecast to become a hurricane by Thursday evening, but it was expected to strengthen and make landfall as a major hurricane (Category 3 or higher) on Friday. Worse yet, once the storm moved inland, it was forecast to eventually stall and meander over South or Southeast Texas for days. Thus, Major Hurricane Harvey was not only forecast to produce devastating winds, but extremely heavy and excessive rainfall, producing devastating and historic flooding over areas especially east of the center of circulation.

Harvey made landfall along the Middle Texas Coast at about 10 PM CDT Friday August 25th as a strong Category 4 hurricane, with estimated maximum sustained winds of 130 mph. The storm produced a catastrophic storm surge of 6 to 12 feet above normal tide levels along the coastline. The Houston metropolitan area, home to approximately 6.7 million residents, experienced unprecedented flooding, with some locations receiving more than 60 inches of rainfall over a four-day period.

The United States Coast Guard closed multiple ports along the Gulf Coast on August 25, 2017. The Port of Houston, the largest port in the United States by foreign waterborne tonnage, was forced to close all terminals from August 25 through August 31, 2017. The Houston Ship Channel, a critical 52-mile waterway connecting the Port of Houston to Galveston Bay, was shut down for six days due to dangerous water levels and debris.

NOAA's National Hurricane Center estimated total damages from Hurricane Harvey at approximately $125 billion, making it one of the costliest natural disasters in United States history. The storm directly caused 68 deaths in Texas.
