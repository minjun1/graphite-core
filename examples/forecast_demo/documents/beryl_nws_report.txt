EXCERPT:

Hurricane Beryl made landfall near Matagorda, Texas on July 8, 2024 as a Category 1 hurricane. The storm brought coastal flooding, flash flooding, widespread wind damage, and confirmed tornadoes across southeast Texas.

The NWS Houston/Galveston office reported 7 direct fatalities and 4 indirect fatalities. One confirmed EF-1 tornado was associated with the event. The highest wind gust recorded was 97 mph near the Brazos River in Brazoria County. Rainfall generally ranged from 4 to 8 inches across the Houston metropolitan area, with localized swaths of 10 to 15 inches in some areas.

Storm surge flooding ranged from 4 to 6 feet along the upper Texas coast, with some areas experiencing up to 8 feet of inundation. Galveston Bay experienced significant storm surge that impacted low-lying coastal infrastructure.

Over 2.7 million households and businesses in the Gulf Coast region, primarily in the Houston metropolitan area, experienced prolonged power outages. CenterPoint Energy, the electric utility serving Houston, reported widespread grid failures. High temperatures exceeding 100°F persisted for several days after the storm, contributing to at least ten deaths related to excess heat or non-functional medical equipment.

The hurricane's center passed just west of Houston, producing extensive damage to residential and commercial structures. Wind damage was widespread across Harris County and surrounding counties.
